# StoryboardFlow Alpha Interview Guide

**Version:** 1.0 (Phase A template)  
**Duration:** 25–35 minutes  
**Format:** Video call (record with consent) or async written responses  

---

## Purpose

Complement quantitative PostHog data with depth on motivation, workflow fit, and unmet needs. Use after the tester completes the alpha test script and Google Form, or as a standalone discovery call.

---

## Preparation

### Interviewer checklist

- [ ] Review tester's PostHog path if available (`app_started` → `project_created` → `first_shot_added` → `export_completed`)
- [ ] Read their Feedback Inbox / form responses
- [ ] Prepare screen share fallback if they need to show the app live
- [ ] Confirm recording consent if recording

### Materials

- Alpha test script (for context)
- Notion Feedback Inbox entry template
- This guide

---

## Introduction (2 min)

> "Thanks for helping us improve StoryboardFlow. There are no right or wrong answers — we're testing the product, not you. I'll ask about your normal workflow and your experience today. Feel free to be candid. May I record this session for internal notes only?"

**Capture:** Consent (yes/no), role, typical storyboard tool today.

---

## Section 1 — Context and workflow (8 min)

1. **Role and projects**  
   "Tell me about the kind of work you do and how storyboards fit in."

2. **Current tools**  
   "What do you use today for storyboards — software, templates, pen and paper?"

3. **Pain points**  
   "What's most frustrating about your current approach?"

4. **Success definition**  
   "When a storyboard session goes well, what does that look like?"

**Probe:** frequency, team size, deliverable format (PDF, presentation, print).

---

## Section 2 — First session replay (10 min)

5. **First impression**  
   "What was your first impression when you opened StoryboardFlow?"

6. **Onboarding**  
   "Was it clear what to do first? Did you feel you needed an account to save work?"

7. **Editing**  
   "Walk me through adding shots and organizing pages. What felt natural? What felt clunky?"

8. **Export**  
   "How did you find export? Was the output what you expected?"

9. **Moments of delight or frustration**  
   "Was there a moment you thought 'this is great' or 'this is annoying'?"

**Probe:** compare to existing tool; note exact UI labels they remember.

---

## Section 3 — Value and fit (8 min)

10. **Comparison**  
    "How does this compare to what you use today — faster, slower, better, worse?"

11. **Missing capabilities**  
    "What's missing that would block you from using this on a real project?"

12. **Willingness to adopt**  
    "On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to use StoryboardFlow for your next storyboard? What would move that number up?"

13. **Recommendation**  
    "Would you recommend it to a colleague in your field? What would you tell them?"

---

## Section 4 — Wrap-up (5 min)

14. **Single biggest improvement**  
    "If we could fix one thing next week, what should it be?"

15. **Anything else**  
    "What didn't I ask that I should know?"

16. **Follow-up**  
    "May we contact you for a second session after we ship improvements?"

---

## Post-interview actions

1. Log summary in **Feedback Inbox** (Source: `Interview`).
2. Create **UX Issues** for reproducible problems.
3. Create **Feature Requests** for net-new ideas (dedupe against existing).
4. Update **Test Users** — set `Interview Done` = yes.
5. Link verbatim quotes to themes for weekly review.

---

## Analysis tags (apply after call)

| Tag | When to use |
|-----|-------------|
| Onboarding | Welcome, auth, project picker |
| Editing | Shots, pages, layout, themes |
| Export | PDF, PNG, quality, discoverability |
| Sync | Cloud, offline, conflicts |
| Billing | Plans, limits, upgrade prompts |

---

## Anti-patterns (avoid)

- Leading questions ("You loved the drag-and-drop, right?")
- Defending design choices during the call
- Skipping negative feedback to save time
- Combining interview with live facilitation unless planned
